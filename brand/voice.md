# Voice

Auris Vive has a very specific voice. It is quiet, confident, and poetic without being precious. It never shouts. It never explains itself defensively. It speaks the way music feels — with presence, not performance.

---

## The voice in four words

**Quiet. Precise. Luminous. Present.**

---

## What Auris Vive sounds like

**Quiet confidence** — statements without explanation. "Every song has a shape." Not "Every song has a shape, and Auris Vive helps you see it." The first sentence trusts the listener. The second doesn't.

**Poetic precision** — specific language, not vague impressionism. "Hear the bass separate from the chord" not "experience the music in a whole new way." Vague wellness language is the enemy.

**Earned wonder** — wonder that comes from what the product actually does, not from the copy writer trying to generate it. If the product is extraordinary, the copy should describe it plainly and let the user feel the wonder themselves.

**Sensory intelligence** — Auris Vive lives at the intersection of the body and the mind. Language should acknowledge both. "The music becomes something you can rest inside" is both physical and intellectual.

## Voice examples

### App Store description

**Wrong:**
> Auris Vive is an revolutionary AI-powered music visualisation experience that transforms the way you hear and see music forever. Using cutting-edge machine learning, it separates audio into stems and creates beautiful real-time visuals.

**Right:**
> Auris Vive listens deeper than you can. It separates any song into its instruments, transcribes every note, and renders living visuals in real time. See what makes a chord feel the way it does. Watch a bass line move. Let the music become an environment.

---

### Onboarding — first screen

**Wrong:**
> Welcome to Auris Vive! Get ready to experience music like never before.

**Right:**
> The music was always this alive.

---

### Empty state — no song playing

**Wrong:**
> Play a song to get started!

**Right:**
> Play something.

---

### Focus mode description

**Wrong:**
> Use Focus Mode for studying, meditation, stress relief, and sleep. Our AI creates a calming visual environment tailored to your music.

**Right:**
> The music slows down visually. A single instrument fills the space. The rest recedes. Some people use this to focus. Some to sleep. Some just to breathe.

---

### Error state — processing failed

**Wrong:**
> Error: Unable to process audio file. Please try again.

**Right:**
> Something went wrong with that one. Try another track.

## What Auris Vive never sounds like

| Avoid | Because |
|-------|---------|
| "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "next-level" | Hype. Auris Vive doesn't need to claim it's extraordinary — it shows it. |
| "Experience music in a whole new way" | Vague. Every music app says this. |
| "Powered by AI" as a feature claim | The AI is infrastructure, not the product. |
| Wellness clichés — "journey", "transformation", "holistic" | Borrowed language. Auris Vive has its own. |
| Exclamation marks | Too loud. The product is calm. The copy should be too. |
| Second-person cheerleading — "You'll love..." | Presumptuous. Let the user decide. |

---

## Naming conventions in copy

- The product is always **Auris Vive** — two words, capital A, capital V. Never "AurisVive", "auris vive", or just "Auris".
- The four stems are always lowercase: **vocals, bass, drums, other**
- The visual layer is "the visuals" or "the visual layer" — never "the visualiser" or "the animation"
- The AI processing is just "listening" in consumer copy — never "machine learning", "neural network", "model"

## Tone by context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| App UI | Minimal. Trust the product. Almost no copy. |
| Onboarding | One sentence per screen. Poetic. No instructions. |
| App Store | Plain language. Describe what it does. Wonder comes from the description, not the adjectives. |
| Marketing / social | More latitude for poetic language. Still never hype. |
| Error states | Human, brief, never apologetic |
| Documentation (this repo) | Clear, direct, no jargon where plain language works |
